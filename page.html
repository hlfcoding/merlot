Merlot–2015

Welcome to the Merlot

Introduction

We both know that the original Merlot's pretty awesome. Why not update it to be even more awesome? Merlot /= Sass; Merlot += contentStyling; Merlot -= imageRequests; Merlot *= responsiveness; So much awesome in one place. Keep reading for more details.

Also, hopefully you'll notice the <header>, #main_content, and <footer> all look slightly different. That's because images are no longer used besides for icons and more complex graphics. The <hr> below still uses images, but those images are now transparent and based on a Sketch file.

Text Blocks

This is a block quote. It hasn't changed much. Except maybe its font-size isn't doubled for mobile viewports, like with most of the other elements.

You may have also noticed Georgia is no longer a body font. Instead, and along with Open Sans, it's been replaced with Merriweather. Also being loaded as preferred is Inconsolata, due to its compactness. The fancy, scripty text is also no longer Georgia, but Gentium Book Basic.

Lists

Unordered
  • Lists remain mostly the same.
  • One change is top-level list items no longer get indented.
    • This should save some precious space in this relatively narrow layout.
  • The chevron remains an image, so new colors mean image editing.
Ordered
  1. Vendor-prefixing is now handled by Autoprefixer.
  2. When possible, the remaining styles have been heavily refactored into Sass code.
